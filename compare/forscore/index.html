Melopal vs forScore

Melopal is built for teaching. forScore is superb for gigs.

If you already use forScore in lessons, you are exactly the kind of teacher Melopal was designed for. Keep your iPad on the stand. Use Melopal for what happens next: assignments, instructions, recordings and student practice.

Melopal vs forScore in short

  • Use forScore if you need a performance-ready digital music stand on iPad.
  • Use Melopal if you need to send students sheet music, practice instructions and recordings after lessons.
  • Use both if you teach from iPad and want students to leave every lesson knowing exactly what to practice.

When to use what app

Melopal is built around the 1-1 music lesson. It starts from a different question: how does the student know what to practice after today's lesson? That means the product is organised around students, assignments, attached instructions, recordings, reference links and a practice view students can actually find again.

forScore is built around the musician. It is one of the strongest ways to read, annotate, organise and perform from PDF sheet music on iPad. If your main need is a stable digital music stand for rehearsals, gigs, page turns and personal score management, forScore is a very strong fit.

The difference is not that one app is good and the other is bad. They are aimed at different parts of a teacher's week.

A one-to-one teaching situation

Here is the practical difference in a normal teacher-student lesson. The teacher has sheet music open, makes markings, gives spoken instructions, maybe adds a YouTube reference, and then needs the student to leave with everything in one place.

Teach from the sheet music Works, but Melopal is not trying to be the main performance stand. Excellent. forScore is built for reading from an iPad music stand during the lesson.
Assign sheet music to the student Choose the student, attach the piece, and create an assignment. You can export or share a PDF, but there is no simple teacher-to-student assignment path.
Make annotations in the sheet music You can add markings and decide what the student receives with the assignment. Excellent. Markings, bowings, fingerings and notes on the score are a major forScore strength.

Help the student find it later The student opens Melopal and sees the current practice work in one place. Not really the job forScore was designed to do.

Where forScore is strong

forScore has had years to become a mature iPad music stand. If your work is performance-focused with sheet music on stage, these strengths matter.

  • Performance oriented and musician-first reading experience
  • Professional annotation tools
  • Bluetooth page turning and stage-friendly workflows
  • Setlists, bookmarks and personal score organisation
  • A digital musicstand built for musicians on stage

Where Melopal is different

Melopal starts where a music stand app usually stops. It is built for the moment when the lesson becomes home practice.

  • Students have their own practice workspace.
  • Assignments connect the PDF, instructions, recordings and references.
  • Young students can join with a QR code and do not need an email address.
  • Parents can see what was assigned without digging through old messages.
  • Teachers can see what each student is currently working on.

That is why Melopal is a better fit for the teaching workflow, while forScore remains the better fit for performing with an iPad on stage. Melopal is not trying to beat forScore at being forScore.

Can you use both?

Absolutely. For many iPad teachers, that is the most natural setup.

Use forScore during the lesson if it is already your trusted music stand. Use Melopal to import or organise the teaching copy, create the assignment and send students the music, instructions and recordings in one place. You do not have to rebuild the way you teach just to make the student's week clearer.

FAQ

Does Melopal replace forScore?

No. Melopal does not try to replace forScore as a digital music stand. It handles the teaching workflow around the lesson: assignments, instructions, recordings and student access.

Can I import my forScore library?

Yes. Melopal supports forScore import so you can bring your existing sheet music and collections into a reusable teaching library. This way you don't have to start from scratch on a new platform. We know how valuable this is for a busy educator and musician.

Which app should I use during lessons?

If forScore already works well on your stand, keep using it. Melopal is for making sure the student has the right assignment after the lesson.

Can students use forScore instead?

Some advanced students might use forScore for their own libraries. Most students need something simpler: this week's music, your instructions and the recordings in one place. That is the Melopal student view.

Can I use both apps long-term?

Yes. The cleanest setup for many teachers is forScore for their own iPad stand and Melopal for student assignments between lessons.
