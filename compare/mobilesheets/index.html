Melopal vs MobileSheets

Melopal is built for teaching. MobileSheets is strong for Android sheet music.

If you already use MobileSheets as your own music library, Melopal is designed for what happens next: assignments, instructions, recordings and student practice.

Melopal vs MobileSheets in short

  • Use Melopal if your main need is sending assignments, instructions and recordings to students.
  • Use MobileSheets if your main need is managing and reading your own PDF sheet music library.
  • Use both if you want a personal performance library and a separate teaching workflow for students.

When to use what app

Melopal is built around the 1-1 music lesson. It starts from a different question: how does the student know what to practice after today's lesson? That means the product is organised around students, assignments, attached instructions, recordings, reference links and a practice view students can actually find again.

MobileSheets is built around the musician's own sheet music library. It is especially strong on Android, with mature tools for reading, annotating, organising PDFs, editing metadata and building setlists.

The difference is not that one app is good and the other is bad. They are aimed at different parts of a teacher's week.

A one-to-one teaching situation

Here is the practical difference in a normal teacher-student lesson. The teacher has sheet music open, makes markings, gives spoken instructions, maybe adds a YouTube reference, and then needs the student to leave with everything in one place.

Where MobileSheets is strong

MobileSheets is a mature, feature-rich sheet music manager. If your work is focused on managing and reading your own sheet music library, these strengths matter.

  • Deep PDF library management
  • Strong Android tablet support
  • Metadata, filtering and organisation tools
  • Setlists and performance-oriented workflows
  • Annotation tools for a musician's own scores

Where Melopal is different

Melopal treats the student as part of the workflow. That changes the shape of the product.

  • Every assignment belongs to a student or group.
  • The PDF, teacher instructions, recordings and links stay together.
  • Students join for free through a QR code or invite link.
  • Parents can understand what was assigned without learning a teacher's file system.
  • Teachers can see what is active for each student.

That is why Melopal is a better fit for the teaching workflow, while MobileSheets remains the better fit for managing a personal Android sheet music library. Melopal is not trying to beat MobileSheets at being MobileSheets.

Can you use both?

Absolutely. For many teachers, that is the most natural setup.

Use MobileSheets for your own library if it is already your trusted music stand. Use Melopal to create the assignment and send students the music, instructions and recordings in one place. You do not have to rebuild the way you teach just to make the student's week clearer.

Students can use Melopal in the browser today, and the Android app is in the making.

FAQ

Does Melopal replace MobileSheets?

No. MobileSheets is a strong personal sheet music manager. Melopal is for assigning music and practice instructions to students.

Which app is better for Android sheet music?

For managing and reading your own sheet music on Android, MobileSheets is very strong. For student assignments and teacher-student workflow, Melopal is built for that specific job. MobileSheets is not.

Can students use MobileSheets instead of Melopal?

They could, but the apps are built for different jobs. MobileSheets is designed as a personal sheet music library. Melopal is built around the teacher-student workflow, keeping sheet music, practice instructions, recordings and feedback together in one place.

Does Melopal work on Android?

The Android app is in the making, but Melopal already works in browsers on Android and all other platforms.

Can I use both apps long-term?

Yes. You can use MobileSheets for gigs and Melopal for student interactions and assignments.
