Melopal vs MobileSheets

MobileSheets organises your library. Melopal organises your teaching.

MobileSheets is one of the strongest sheet music managers available, especially for Android users. Melopal solves a different problem: helping students receive the right music, instructions and recordings after each lesson.

Short answer

  • Use MobileSheets if your main need is managing and reading your own PDF sheet music library.
  • Use Melopal if your main need is sending assignments, instructions and recordings to students.
  • Use both if you want a personal performance library and a separate teaching workflow for students.

Who each app is for

MobileSheets is built for musicians who want a powerful digital sheet music library. It is especially well known among Android users, and it gives performers deep tools for organising PDFs, editing metadata, building setlists and reading music from a tablet.

Melopal is built for teachers who need to turn lessons into practice. The library matters, but the real purpose is what happens with the music: which student receives it, what they should practise, what recording they should hear, and where they will find it again at home.

If your problem is "I need my own PDFs organised," MobileSheets may be the right tool. If your problem is "my students cannot find what I assigned," Melopal is aimed at that.

A typical lesson using Melopal and MobileSheets

  1. Use MobileSheets to read from your own organised PDF library if that is your preferred stand.
  2. Teach the lesson and decide what the student should practise next week.
  3. Open Melopal and choose the student.
  4. Attach the relevant sheet music from your Melopal teaching library.
  5. Add written instructions with bars, tempo and what to listen for.
  6. Add a reference recording or a short teacher recording.
  7. Send the assignment before the student leaves.
  8. The student opens one practice view instead of searching through email, chats or cloud folders.

MobileSheets can be excellent for your own library. Melopal is there when the music needs to become a student assignment.

Where MobileSheets is stronger

MobileSheets is a mature, feature-rich sheet music manager. If you mostly need a personal PDF library, these strengths are real.

  • Deep PDF library management
  • Strong Android tablet support
  • Metadata, filtering and organisation tools
  • Setlists and performance-oriented workflows
  • Annotation tools for a musician's own scores

Melopal is not trying to become the most advanced personal PDF manager. It is deliberately focused on music teaching and student practice.

Where Melopal is different

Melopal treats the student as part of the workflow. That changes the shape of the product.

  • Every assignment belongs to a student or group.
  • The PDF, teacher instructions, recordings and links stay together.
  • Students join for free through a QR code or invite link.
  • Parents can understand what was assigned without learning a teacher's file system.
  • Teachers can see what is active for each student.

For a private teaching studio, the problem is rarely "I have no way to store PDFs." The problem is usually "I need every student to know exactly what to practise." That is where Melopal is different.

Can you use both?

Yes. If MobileSheets is already your personal library and you like it, there is no reason to stop using it for yourself.

Melopal can sit beside it as the teaching layer. Use MobileSheets to manage and read your own music. Use Melopal when you want to assign music to a student, add instructions, attach recordings and make the week of practice clear.

Students can use Melopal in the browser today, and the Android app is in the making.

FAQ

Does Melopal replace MobileSheets?

No. MobileSheets is a strong personal sheet music manager. Melopal is for assigning music and practice instructions to students.

Which app is better for Android sheet music?

For managing and reading your own sheet music on Android, MobileSheets is very strong. For student assignments and teacher-student workflow, Melopal is built for that specific job.

Can students use MobileSheets instead?

Some advanced students may manage their own libraries. Most students need a simpler place that only shows the current assignment, instructions and recordings.

Does Melopal work on Android?

Melopal works in the browser on Android devices now. The Android app is in the making.

Can I use both apps long-term?

Yes. Use MobileSheets for your own performance library and Melopal for student assignments and practice between lessons.
